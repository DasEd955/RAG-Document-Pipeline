    I have a current freshman daughter residing in West Halls. She's looking for off-campus housing now for next year when she's a sophomore. Appears that's needed to be done between now and November.

    So not knowing the area, good, bad or indifferent, I'm reaching out to trip advisor.

    My son is a sophomore living in West Halls but planning to remain there next year (he's got a single and loves it). So, no real context on actual experience with various landlords, etc., however, have you tried: http://studentaffairs.psu.edu/offcampus

    There's a variety of links to resources, references to housing fairs, etc. all of which would seem like they might give your daughter some local resources and fellow students from which she might be able to garner some opinions and guidance.

    Bus service is excellent up there, so anything which is on one of the bus routes will certainly provide a good transportation option -- everything pretty much runs onto campus with stops at the Library and downtown (where picking up the blue/white campus loops is simple). Combine that with a commuter meal plan (for lunch, etc. so she doesn't have to run back to apartment to eat on busy days), and you probably have a good start.

    He's a part of an SLO (special living option) -- in his case the IST (Information Science and Technology) SLO in Thompson -- Engineering is also over in West in McKee and I think several others. Those in an SLO who participate in their floor's activities throughout the year and earn sufficient participation credit and apply for a dorm contract are essentially guaranteed a contract renewal (and in fact, their room if they are staying in a single or with the same roommate). It's nice deal really.

    http://www.housing.psu.edu/housing/housing/special-living-options.cfm

    5. Re: Off-Campus Housing
    12 years ago

    I graduated from Penn State in 2012, lived on-campus for three years and off-campus for two years.

    For the two years, we rented from The Apartment Store - http://www.apartmentstore.com/state-college/. Not the greatest management company ever (in fact, I and everyone else I know has a long list of complaints), but the properties are decent and in good locations. We lived in Nicholas Tower (big party building, also not sure if it has apartments for only two people) and Park Hill (I had a one-bedroom with one other roommate - decent place).

    At this point though, though I can't quite remember the timeline, many of the nicer, close-to-campus apartments will be all booked up. Though obviously I don't know your daughter, I would recommend she stay on campus for another year. I find that most sophomores still live on-campus and the ones who move off-campus get somewhat isolated from their peers. Especially if she likes a quiet place to live, a dorm is going to be way more quiet (and regulated to keep it quiet) than an apartment building. If she stays on campus for another year, she would have some more time to see where her friends live and find some places she likes downtown. I just personally know (and I'm quite independent and social) that I wouldn't have been ready for an apartment as a sophomore - especially without a car (no way to get to Walmart/get groceries, etc.).

    Regarding the lottery, though it has changed a bit probably since I lived in campus, there were separate lotteries for regular on-campus housing, Nittany Apartments, Eastview Terrace (where I lived my junior year). So there is a pretty good chance of getting housing if you try all the different housing options lotteries.

    I hope this helps - and hope your daughter is having a great time up there!
